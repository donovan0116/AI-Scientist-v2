# Title: Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning: Coordination, Scalability, and Sample Efficiency

## Keywords
multi-agent reinforcement learning, MARL, coordination, emergent behavior, decentralized control

## TL;DR
Explore how multiple agents can learn to cooperate or compete in shared environments—addressing credit assignment, scalability, and sample efficiency in MARL.

## Abstract
Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) studies how multiple autonomous agents learn to make sequential decisions in a shared environment, with applications ranging from robotics swarms and game AI to traffic control and distributed resource allocation. Unlike single-agent RL, MARL must address non-stationarity (other agents’ policies change during learning), credit assignment (who deserves reward in team settings), and scalability (curse of dimensionality in joint action spaces). This topic focuses on empirical and methodological contributions that improve coordination, sample efficiency, or scalability in MARL—including but not limited to centralized training with decentralized execution (CTDE), communication and message passing, emergent cooperation in mixed cooperative–competitive settings, and transfer from simulation to real-world multi-agent systems. We welcome ideas that can be validated through concrete experiments (e.g., standard MARL benchmarks such as StarCraft II, Multi-Agent Particle World, or custom environments), with clear hypotheses and measurable outcomes suitable for workshop-level automated discovery and paper generation.
